ABOUT US

Hinkro Kente designs and hand-weaves bespoke kente textiles, crafted to each client's unique vision. We combine authentic Ghanaian weaving tradition with refined design to create one-of-a-kind luxury pieces for weddings, special events, and heritage celebrations.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Kente cloth or fabric ?

Kente cloth is a traditional Ghanaian textile made from silk, cotton, rayon or polyester known for its vibrant colors and intricate, geometric patterns. Each design has cultural significance, representing history, social status, and values. Originally woven by the Ashanti and Ewe people, Kente is often worn during important ceremonies and events, symbolizing heritage, prestige, and pride. To learn more click here

How are Kente cloth made ?

Kente cloth is made by weaving narrow strips of fabric on a loom, combining silk or cotton threads into intricate patterns. These strips are then sewn together to create larger pieces. The process requires skilled craftsmanship and attention to detail to achieve the vibrant designs. To lean more click here

What is the price for Kente cloth ?

At Hinkro, the price of Kente cloth can vary widely depending on factors such as the complexity of the pattern, the materials used (silk or cotton), and the size of the fabric. Typically, Kente ranges from around $70 to several hundred dollars per piece, with authentic handwoven Kente costing more than wax print versions. Shop from our curated Kente fabrics for you.

Do you produce original or authentic Kente for Engagement ?

Yes, Hinkro Kente produces authentic, handwoven Kente cloth, combining traditional craftsmanship with modern design techniques to ensure both originality and high quality in every piece. Learn more here on identifying the difference between Kente and wax print fabrics.

Where are you located ?

Hinkro Kente is based in Ghana's capital, Accra, specifically in Ablekuma. Our weaving studio is located there. We also have a presence in Atlanta, Georgia, in the United States, and Leicester, in the United Kingdom.

What are the steps in acquiring a kente from you ?

Customers begin the process by booking an appointment with Hinkro for a fee of $50, during which they discuss their preferred colors, patterns, and design requirements; select from existing designs or request a custom-made pattern; receive a price quote based on their selections; and finally, wait for the handcrafted Kente to be produced and delivered.
